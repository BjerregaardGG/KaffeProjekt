The Flat Whites' history

The Flat White, a popular espresso-based coffee drink, has a history that is often debated between Australia and New Zealand, with both countries claiming its origin.

A Flat White is made with a double shot of espresso and steamed milk, similar to a latte but with a smaller volume and a velvety microfoam on top. The texture is creamy, and it often has a more concentrated coffee flavor than a latte because of the smaller milk ratio.
